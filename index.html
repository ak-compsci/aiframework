Our Vision for AI in Education

To empower every Alaskan student and educator to ethically and effectively engage with Artificial Intelligence, fostering critical thinking, creativity, and responsible digital citizenship, thereby providing all learners with the opportunity and comprehensive support to thrive in an evolving, AI-driven world.

Guiding Principles

Human-Centered

AI must augment human capabilities, critical thinking, and creativity, never replacing human judgment or decision-making in educational contexts. All AI use should begin with human inquiry and culminate in human reflection and insight.

Fair Access

AI tools and education should be accessible to all students, actively mitigating broadband disparities and device availability challenges across Alaska's diverse regions.

Transparency

The functionalities, limitations, and potential biases of AI tools must be clearly communicated to all stakeholders. Explicit disclosure is suggested when AI is used in educational materials or assessments.

Oversight

Clear lines of responsibility for AI tool selection and outcomes are vital. Robust human oversight is mandatory for any decisions that directly impact students, such as grading or disciplinary actions.

Security

Rigorous protection of student data and digital well-being is non-negotiable. This includes protocols against unauthorized sharing of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and compliance with local, state, and federal laws.

Ethical Use

Promote responsible and ethical engagement with AI, fostering an understanding of intellectual property rights, preventing misuse, and building critical evaluation skills for all AI-generated content.

Cultural Responsiveness

AI tools should be critically evaluated and used to affirm, rather than diminish, the diverse cultures of Alaska. Proactive measures must be taken to mitigate algorithmic biases and prevent misinterpretation, misappropriation, or the exclusion of crucial local context.

Guidance for District & School Leaders

This section provides a strategic roadmap for district and school leaders to develop, implement, and sustain AI policies. It focuses on creating a robust governance structure, ensuring legal compliance, and managing the procurement of AI tools effectively. The goal is to build a systemic approach to AI integration that is safe, equitable, and aligned with your educational mission.

Establish a cross-functional AI policy team with diverse stakeholders including teachers, curriculum leaders, IT staff, and administrators. This group will guide the development of a comprehensive strategy. You can find more information on this process in the TeachAI Guidance for Schools Toolkit.

Develop a clear vision for AI integration that aligns with your district’s educational goals. The vision should emphasize a balanced, human-centered approach that augments human capabilities without replacing essential judgment or interaction.

Create a robust process for evaluating AI tools for safety, efficacy, and legal compliance (FERPA, COPPA, CIPA). This includes a vendor vetting checklist to ensure that all tools adhere to high data privacy and security standards before adoption. For a practical checklist, see the Washington OSPI Human-Centered AI Guidance.

Develop a comprehensive AI Literacy program that provides all staff and students with an understanding of AI fundamentals, capabilities, and ethical use. This prepares everyone to engage with AI responsibly. The TeachAI AI Literacy Framework provides an excellent starting point.

Integrate the AI guidance into your existing district policies, such as the technology use policy, student code of conduct, and academic integrity policies, to ensure consistency and a streamlined implementation process.

Recognize that AI is a rapidly evolving field. Establish a mechanism for ongoing research and evaluation of AI initiatives, including feedback from all stakeholders, to ensure your policies and practices remain relevant and effective over time.

AI EdTech Vendor Vetting Checklist

The market for AI EdTech is expanding rapidly. A structured evaluation process is critical to ensure any tool used in your district is safe, effective, and compliant. Use this checklist to ask vendors the right questions before procurement.

Business Alignment & Strategy
+
  • How does this AI solution align with our district's educational goals?
  • What are the long-term scalability requirements?
  • What key metrics will measure the impact on learning outcomes?
Technical Due Diligence
+
  • What is the source of the AI models (in-house, third-party)?
  • How do you ensure model explainability and mitigate bias?
  • What types of training data were used?
  • Do you employ human review for automated decisions?
Data Governance & Privacy Standards
+
  • Can you provide evidence of compliance with FERPA, COPPA, and other privacy laws?
  • What are your practices for anonymizing personal information?
  • Do your systems support user rights for data access, correction, and deletion?
Support, Integration, & Flexibility
+
  • What Service Level Agreements (SLAs) do you offer?
  • What onboarding and training resources are provided for educators?
  • How easily can your solution integrate with our existing technology stack?
Legal & Contractual
+
  • What are the ownership terms for input data and AI-generated outputs?
  • Do contracts prevent you from using our confidential information for training?
  • Do terms mandate regular audits and bias monitoring?

Guidance for Teachers

This section provides practical guidance for educators on integrating AI into the classroom. It focuses on leveraging AI to enhance instruction, save time, and foster student creativity, all while navigating the new realities of academic integrity. The resources here are designed to empower you with the confidence and tools to use AI effectively and responsibly.

The `H→AI→H` Framework for Learning

As noted in the Washington OSPI guidance, a human-centered approach to AI in education should always start with human inquiry and end with human reflection. AI is a powerful tool to be used in the middle of this process, augmenting and streamlining your work without replacing your professional judgment.

AI Acceptable Use Scale for Assignments

This interactive scale provides a clear framework for defining how AI can be used for different assignments. Communicating the specific level to students in advance reduces ambiguity and promotes academic honesty. Click on a level to explore its details.

Level 0: No AI
Level 1: AI-Assisted Planning
Level 2: AI-Assisted Drafting
Level 3: AI-Collaborative Creation
Level 4: Full AI (Human Evaluation)
Level 0: No AI
Description:

The assessment is completed entirely without AI assistance in a controlled environment, ensuring that students rely solely on their existing knowledge and skills.

Best For:

Testing foundational knowledge, ensuring authenticity of individual student capabilities, in-class supervised work.

Examples:

In-class essays, traditional exams, handwritten assignments.

Citation Requirement:

No AI use permitted.

Level 1: AI-Assisted Planning
Description:

AI may be used for pre-task activities such as brainstorming, outlining, and initial research. This level focuses on the effective use of AI for planning and ideation, but final submissions should be entirely human-authored.

Best For:

Overcoming writer's block, exploring diverse perspectives, initial project concept development.

Examples:

Using AI to generate a list of research topics, structuring an essay outline.

Citation Requirement:

Students must disclose AI use for idea generation or outlining in a footnote or as guided by the teacher.

Level 2: AI-Assisted Drafting
Description:

AI can be used to help draft parts of the task, including initial versions or specific sections. Students should critically evaluate and significantly revise the AI-suggested outputs to make them their own.

Best For:

Organizing complex information, improving logical flow, enhancing clarity of presentation, and providing feedback on writing.

Examples:

Using AI to refine a paragraph's structure, generating suggestions for synonyms or improving sentence flow.

Citation Requirement:

Students must disclose AI use for drafting and editing, specifying the nature of assistance.

Level 3: AI-Collaborative Creation
Description:

AI-generated content can be included as a core part of the assignment. The assessment judges the student's ability to critically evaluate and effectively integrate AI outputs into their own work.

Best For:

Tasks where the ability to synthesize and critique is a key learning outcome, exploring and refining AI capabilities.

Examples:

Critiquing an AI-generated report, co-creating a story with AI and reflecting on the process, using AI to synthesize research and then writing a reflection on its limitations.

Citation Requirement:

Full disclosure of AI generation is required, including prompts used and a reflection on the outputs.

Level 4: Full AI (Human Evaluation)
Description:

AI is used creatively to enhance problem-solving, generate novel insights, or develop innovative solutions. The student's role is to act as the prompter and evaluator, directing the AI to achieve the assessment's goals.

Best For:

Tasks where AI is integral to the learning outcomes, promoting critical evaluation and responsible use of AI as a tool.

Examples:

Using AI to generate a detailed architectural design and then justifying its strengths and weaknesses, or developing a marketing campaign using AI-generated content and explaining the strategic decisions.

Citation Requirement:

Extensive disclosure of AI use is required, including the full conversation with the AI.

Practical AI Use Cases for the Classroom

AI can significantly support teachers in developing and delivering more effective and personalized lessons, and in streamlining administrative tasks. Here are some ways AI can be a valuable tool for you, so you can spend more time on high-value interactions with students.

To Enhance Instruction:
  • Lesson Planning: Generate ideas, objectives, and activities for any subject.
  • Content Creation: Create reading passages, practice problems, or visual aid descriptions.
  • Differentiation: Adapt materials for varying reading levels or learning needs.
  • Rubric Generation: Quickly draft clear and comprehensive rubrics for assignments.
To Save Time:
  • Drafting Communications: Write emails to parents, class newsletters, or announcements.
  • Formative Feedback: Provide initial feedback on student drafts (with human oversight).
  • Administrative Tasks: Organize schedules, create reports, or manage inventory lists.
  • Brainstorming: Overcome creative blocks for project ideas or discussion prompts.

Guidance for Students & Families

This section helps students and families understand what AI is, how it can be used as a powerful learning tool, and how to use it responsibly. The goal is to build AI literacy and good digital citizenship habits, preparing students to navigate an AI-driven world safely and ethically.

How You Can Use AI for Learning

  • 🧠
    Be a Thought Partner

    Use AI to brainstorm ideas, explore different perspectives on a topic, or get help when you're stuck on a problem.

  • 📝
    Get Personalized Feedback

    Ask AI to explain a difficult concept in a different way or provide feedback on your writing before you submit it.

  • 🎨
    Boost Your Creativity

    Collaborate with AI to create stories, poems, or images, and learn how to evaluate and revise AI-generated content.

The Golden Rules of AI Use

  • 🚫
    Protect Your Personal Information

    NEVER enter sensitive personal info (your full name, address, passwords, or student ID) into public AI tools.

  • 💡
    Your Brain Must Do the Work

    AI is a helper, not a replacement for your own thinking. Submitting AI work as your own is plagiarism.

  • ✅
    Always Fact-Check AI

    AI can make mistakes or "hallucinate" facts. Always verify important information with reliable sources.

  • 🗣️
    Cite Your Source

    If your teacher allows AI use, you must disclose how you used it. Honesty is key to academic integrity.
