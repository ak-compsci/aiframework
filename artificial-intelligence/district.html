Guidance for District & School Leaders

This section provides a strategic roadmap for district and school leaders to develop, implement, and sustain AI policies. It focuses on creating a robust governance structure, ensuring legal compliance, and managing the procurement of AI tools effectively. The goal is to build a systemic approach to AI integration that is safe, equitable, and aligned with your educational mission.

The 6-Step Policy Development Process

Effective AI integration is a continuous journey. Use this guide to navigate the key phases of developing and maintaining relevant, effective AI guidelines. Click each step to expand details.

1

Form a Dedicated Team

+

Establish a cross-functional AI policy team with diverse stakeholders including teachers, curriculum leaders, IT staff, and administrators. This group will guide the development of a comprehensive strategy.

Key Team Members:

  • Curriculum/Instructional Leaders
  • IT/Technology Directors
  • Data Privacy Officers
  • Teacher Representatives
  • Student/Parent Representatives
  • Legal/Compliance Staff

ðŸ“‹ TeachAI Guidance for Schools Toolkit provides detailed guidance on this process.

2

Define a Clear Vision

+

Develop a clear vision for AI integration that aligns with your district's educational goals. The vision should emphasize a balanced, human-centered approach that augments human capabilities without replacing essential judgment or interaction.

Vision Components:

  • Alignment with district mission and values
  • Clear statement of AI's role in education
  • Commitment to equity and accessibility
  • Emphasis on human oversight and judgment
  • Focus on student learning outcomes
3

Establish Vetting & Procurement

+

Create a robust process for evaluating AI tools for safety, efficacy, and legal compliance (FERPA, COPPA, CIPA). This includes a vendor vetting checklist to ensure that all tools adhere to high data privacy and security standards before adoption.

Evaluation Criteria:

  • Educational alignment and effectiveness
  • Data privacy and security compliance
  • Technical reliability and support
  • Cost and scalability considerations
  • Integration with existing systems

ðŸ“‹ See the Washington OSPI Human-Centered AI Guidance for a practical checklist.

4

Implement AI Literacy Training

+

Develop a comprehensive AI Literacy program that provides all staff and students with an understanding of AI fundamentals, capabilities, and ethical use. This prepares everyone to engage with AI responsibly.

Training Components:

  • AI basics and terminology
  • Practical AI tools for education
  • Ethical considerations and bias awareness
  • Data privacy and security best practices
  • Academic integrity in the AI age

ðŸ“‹ The TeachAI AI Literacy Framework provides an excellent starting point.

5

Integrate Policies Locally

+

Integrate the AI guidance into your existing district policies, such as the technology use policy, student code of conduct, and academic integrity policies, to ensure consistency and a streamlined implementation process.

Policy Integration Areas:

  • Acceptable Use Policy updates
  • Academic integrity guidelines
  • Data governance policies
  • Professional development requirements
  • Student handbook revisions
6

Plan for Continuous Review

+

Recognize that AI is a rapidly evolving field. Establish a mechanism for ongoing research and evaluation of AI initiatives, including feedback from all stakeholders, to ensure your policies and practices remain relevant and effective over time.

Review Process:

  • Quarterly policy team meetings
  • Annual comprehensive policy review
  • Stakeholder feedback collection
  • Technology trend monitoring
  • Legal and compliance updates

AI EdTech Vendor Vetting Checklist

The market for AI EdTech is expanding rapidly. A structured evaluation process is critical to ensure any tool used in your district is safe, effective, and compliant. Use this comprehensive checklist to evaluate AI tools before implementation.

ðŸ“‹ Download: AI Vendor Evaluation Checklist - Page Format | AI Tool Evaluation Checklist - Table Format

1

Business Alignment & Strategy

+

Does this AI tool align with your educational goals and district strategy?

Key Questions to Consider:

  • How does this tool support specific curriculum standards or learning objectives?
  • What educational problem does this solve that existing tools cannot?
  • How will this tool enhance (not replace) teacher instruction?
  • What is the expected return on investment in terms of student outcomes?
  • How does this align with your district's equity and inclusion goals?

Documentation Required: Vendor should provide clear use cases, research on educational effectiveness, and alignment with pedagogical best practices.

2

Technical Due Diligence

+

Understanding the AI technology behind the tool

Critical Technical Areas:

  • AI Model Information: What models are used (GPT, custom, etc.) and how are they trained?
  • Data Sources: What data was used to train the AI and is it appropriate for K-12 education?
  • Bias Mitigation: What steps has the vendor taken to identify and reduce algorithmic bias?
  • Accuracy & Reliability: What is the tool's accuracy rate and how are errors handled?
  • Updates & Maintenance: How frequently is the AI model updated and improved?

Red Flags: Vendors who cannot explain their AI technology, refuse to discuss bias mitigation, or make unrealistic accuracy claims.

3

Data Governance & Privacy

+

Ensuring compliance with FERPA, COPPA, and state data privacy laws

Essential Privacy Protections:

  • FERPA Compliance: Signed FERPA agreements and clear data handling procedures
  • COPPA Compliance: Special protections for students under 13
  • Data Minimization: Tool only collects data necessary for educational purposes
  • Data Retention: Clear policies on how long data is stored and deletion procedures
  • Data Location: Where is student data stored and processed?
  • Third-Party Sharing: Is data shared with any third parties?

Required Documentation:

  • Privacy policy written in plain language
  • Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
  • Security audit reports or certifications
  • Incident response procedures
4

Support & Integration

+

Evaluating implementation support and system compatibility

Implementation Considerations:

  • Training Resources: What professional development is provided for educators?
  • Technical Support: Quality and availability of customer support
  • System Integration: Compatibility with existing LMS, SIS, and other tools
  • Accessibility: Does the tool meet ADA and Section 508 requirements?
  • Multi-language Support: Available in languages spoken by your student population
  • Device Compatibility: Works on devices available to students

Ongoing Support:

  • Response times for technical issues
  • Regular training updates and resources
  • User community or support forums
5

Legal & Contractual

+

Protecting your district through proper legal agreements

Critical Contract Elements:

  • Data Ownership: District retains ownership of all student data
  • Usage Rights: Clear limitations on how vendor can use district data
  • Liability & Indemnification: Vendor liability for data breaches or misuse
  • Termination Clauses: Data return/deletion procedures upon contract end
  • Service Level Agreements: Uptime guarantees and performance standards
  • Intellectual Property: Ownership of student-created content

Financial Considerations:

  • Total cost of ownership including training and support
  • Pricing transparency and scalability
  • Payment terms and cancellation policies
6

Pilot & Evaluation Process

+

Testing AI tools before district-wide implementation

Recommended Pilot Process:

  • Limited Scope: Start with 1-2 schools or classrooms
  • Clear Metrics: Define success criteria before the pilot begins
  • Teacher Feedback: Regular check-ins with participating educators
  • Student Impact: Monitor both academic and engagement outcomes
  • Technical Performance: Track uptime, speed, and user experience

Evaluation Criteria:

  • Ease of use for teachers and students
  • Measurable impact on learning outcomes
  • Integration with existing workflows
  • Technical reliability and performance
  • Value for investment

Timeline: Allow at least 6-8 weeks for a meaningful pilot evaluation with comprehensive feedback collection.

Implementation Guide

Practical steps for rolling out AI policies and practices across your district. This phased approach ensures systematic implementation while allowing for continuous learning and adjustment.

1

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)

+

Establishing the groundwork for successful AI integration

Key Activities:

  • Assemble your AI policy team - Include diverse stakeholders from curriculum, IT, legal, and classroom educators
  • Conduct current state assessment - Inventory existing technology, policies, and staff readiness
  • Develop initial policy framework - Create draft policies aligned with district values and educational goals
  • Stakeholder engagement - Hold listening sessions with teachers, parents, and students
  • Budget planning - Identify funding sources and create implementation budget

Deliverables:

  • AI implementation team roster and charter
  • Current state assessment report
  • Draft AI policy framework
  • Stakeholder feedback summary
  • Implementation budget and timeline
2

Phase 2: Pilot & Training (Months 4-6)

+

Testing approaches with limited scope to learn and refine

Implementation Activities:

  • Launch pilot programs - Select 2-3 schools or departments for initial implementation
  • Professional development rollout - Begin AI literacy training for pilot participants
  • Vendor pilots - Test selected AI tools with small groups
  • Policy refinement - Update policies based on pilot learnings
  • Feedback mechanisms - Establish regular check-ins and data collection

Success Metrics:

  • Teacher confidence and comfort with AI tools
  • Student engagement and learning outcomes
  • Technical performance and reliability
  • Policy effectiveness and clarity
  • Cost-effectiveness of selected tools

Critical: Document lessons learned and best practices for scaling in Phase 3.

3

Phase 3: Scale & Monitor (Months 7-12)

+

District-wide rollout with continuous monitoring and improvement

Scaling Activities:

  • District-wide policy rollout - Implement refined policies across all schools
  • Comprehensive training program - AI literacy for all staff and students
  • Technology deployment - Roll out approved AI tools district-wide
  • Support system activation - Launch help desk, peer support networks, and resources
  • Communication campaign - Keep all stakeholders informed of progress and changes

Monitoring & Evaluation:

  • Regular policy reviews - Quarterly assessment of policy effectiveness
  • Performance tracking - Monitor academic, engagement, and operational metrics
  • Stakeholder feedback - Ongoing surveys and focus groups
  • Technology monitoring - Track usage, performance, and security
  • Continuous improvement - Regular updates based on data and feedback
4

Change Management Strategies

+

Essential strategies for managing the human side of AI implementation

Building Buy-in:

  • Start with willing participants - Identify and support early adopters
  • Showcase quick wins - Highlight early successes and positive outcomes
  • Address concerns directly - Provide clear answers about job security and student privacy
  • Provide adequate support - Ensure training and help are readily available
  • Celebrate progress - Recognize and reward successful implementation

Common Challenges & Solutions:

  • Resistance to change: Focus on benefits and provide hands-on training
  • Technology anxiety: Offer peer support and step-by-step guidance
  • Privacy concerns: Provide transparent information about data protection
  • Workload increases: Phase implementation to allow time for adjustment
  • Inconsistent usage: Create accountability measures and support systems
